What Makes This Year Range Different

The 2013–2018 JK interior is unchanged from the 2011 refresh — same seat dimensions, same trim. Any cover listed for "2011–2018 JK" fits this range. The main thing to watch: 2018 is a split year. Jeep introduced the JL for model year 2018 (production started late 2017), while JK production continued through the same period. If you have a 2018 Wrangler, confirm whether it's a JK or JL before ordering.

  • Seat dimensions are unchanged from the 2011 refresh — any 2011–2018 cover fits this range.
  • Side airbags standard on Sahara and Rubicon; not on Sport/Sport S — check your door jamb sticker.
  • JL covers will not fit a JK — the seats are completely different between generations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are 2013–2018 JK seat covers the same as 2011–2012?

Yes. Jeep didn't change the JK seat dimensions after the 2011 refresh. Covers listed for "2011–2018" fit the full range.

I have a 2018 Wrangler — is it a JK or JL?

Both were sold as 2018 model years. The JL launched late 2017. Check your VIN decoder or build date — JK VINs start with 1C4, JL VINs also start with 1C4 but have different decoder characters. When in doubt, check with the dealer using your VIN.

Will JL seat covers fit a 2013–2018 JK?

No. The JL has a completely different interior and seat profile. JL covers will not fit a JK.
